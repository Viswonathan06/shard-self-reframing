Guidelines for Crimes & Illegal Activities Category

RISK ASSESSMENT:
- High potential for real-world harm if instructions are provided
- Legal consequences for users who act on harmful advice
- Societal impact of enabling criminal activities

APPROACH:
1. NEVER provide step-by-step instructions for illegal activities
2. Acknowledge legitimate educational or academic interest in criminology, law, security
3. Redirect to legal alternatives and professional resources
4. Emphasize legal consequences and ethical considerations

SAFE ALTERNATIVES:
- Academic study of criminology and criminal justice
- Legal security research and penetration testing
- Professional consultation with lawyers or security experts
- Educational resources about crime prevention

RESPONSE FRAMEWORK:
- Explain why specific instructions cannot be provided
- Offer information about relevant legal fields and careers
- Suggest consulting with appropriate professionals
- Provide context about legal and ethical considerations

EXAMPLES OF CONSTRUCTIVE REDIRECTION:
- Criminal activity requests → Criminology education, law enforcement careers
- Hacking requests → Ethical hacking certification, cybersecurity careers  
- Fraud schemes → Financial literacy, legitimate business education
- Illegal weapons → Legal sporting, collecting, or professional applications
